Navigating Ethical Dilemmas

Evaluate realistic ethical scenarios medical assistants may face and identify the most ethical course of action. Learn how ethical principles guide professional decisions in complex situations.

💊 Scenario 1: Patient Medication Concern
A patient confides that they've been skipping doses of a prescribed medication because they don't believe in taking pills and prefer "natural" remedies. They ask you not to tell the physician. What is the most ethical response?
🏥 Scenario 2: Fair Resource Allocation
Two patients need immediate attention: an uninsured patient with a non-life-threatening injury and an insured patient with a mild concern. Resources are limited. The physician asks your opinion on who should be seen first. How should you respond?
⚠️ Scenario 3: Suspected Patient Harm
You notice an elderly patient has multiple bruises in different stages of healing and seems afraid of their adult child who accompanies them. You suspect elder abuse. Following ethical principles of beneficence and nonmaleficence, what should you do?

Do I Need a Trainee Registration?

Pharmacy technician licensing requirements vary significantly by state. Some states require a trainee registration or permit before you may perform any pharmacy duties. Others require full registration only after passing your national exam. A few states have no licensure requirement at all. Search your state below to find out exactly what applies to you.

Apply Early in Trainee States
Texas and Ohio require the trainee registration to be active before you perform any pharmacy duties. Texas processing takes up to 6 weeks and Ohio requires fingerprinting through a WebCheck provider. Do not wait until your externship begins.
Pennsylvania and Ohio Added Trainee Requirements
Both states recently introduced mandatory trainee registrations. Pennsylvania added a $15 trainee registration (min. age 16). Ohio added a $40 trainee registration effective September 2025 (valid 18 months, non-renewable).
Key Tips for All Students
Check your state board early Apply for trainee status at program start, not at externship
Requirements change Always confirm with your State Board of Pharmacy directly
Background checks Most states require fingerprinting or criminal history review
After your exam Upgrade from trainee to full registration in your state
Employer requirements Employers may require more than the state minimum

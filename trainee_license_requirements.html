Pharmacy Technician — Trainee States at a Glance

Several states require students to register as a Pharmacy Technician Trainee with their State Board of Pharmacy before performing any pharmacy duties. This registration is separate from the full license you will obtain after passing the PTCB or NHA ExCPT exam.

Require full license only 34 states
No state licensure required 5 states

Clinical Medical Assistant (CCMA) — State Regulations

Unlike pharmacy, the majority of states have no state licensure requirement for clinical medical assistants. The NHA CCMA national certification is sufficient to satisfy employer requirements in nearly all states.

Require state registration/license Washington State only
Require certification for procedures only ~6 states
No state requirement ~44 states + DC
National certification NHA CCMA

Pharmacy Students — Act Early
States like Texas and Arizona require trainee registration before you may enter a pharmacy for training hours. Check your state's Board of Pharmacy website as soon as your program begins.
Washington State — CCMA Students
Washington requires clinical medical assistants to register with the WA Department of Health under RCW 18.360 before performing clinical procedures. A Medical Assistant Trainee permit is available for students actively enrolled in a training program.
